Research

My PhD research work has been focused on how large scale structure forms.

I’ve studied galaxy clusters in hydrodynamical simulations. In particular I was exploring the discrepancy between the number of galaxy clusters observed and the number expected from N-body simulations. In my paper used the results of the Millennium Gas Simulations to argue that one should include the effects of baryonic physics (supernovae feedback, preheating, AGN, etc.) when computing the number of galaxy clusters expected in a given survey.

I’ve also studied how the initial conditions of N-body simulations affects the growth of structure.

The bulk of my research time has been dedicated to the study of structure formation in modified gravity cosmologies. In particular I wrote an extension to the MPI parallel Gadget-2 code to solve the equations of motion for f(R) gravity.

You you find the pdf of my thesis on the UoM library website.
